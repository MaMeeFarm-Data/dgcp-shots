### System Context

Airports remain among the most visible symbols of modern connectivity.

People travel through airports.

Goods move through airports.

Businesses connect through airports.

Tourism operates through airports.

Global economic activity increasingly depends on aviation networks.

Many observers focus on aircraft, passenger volumes, flight schedules, or travel services.

The discussion frequently centers on aviation activity itself.

The systems enabled by aviation connectivity receive less attention.

While reviewing developments across international trade, tourism, logistics, business travel, and global mobility, I found myself paying less attention to aircraft and more attention to the infrastructure operating through them.

The airport remained visible.

The connectivity infrastructure became visible.

The observation was not about aviation alone.

The observation was about access.
